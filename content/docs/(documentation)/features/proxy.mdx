A **proxy** acts as an intermediary between your scraper and the target website.  
It helps you:

- Mask your real IP address  
- Avoid IP bans during large scraping tasks  
- Access geo-restricted or localized content

MrScraper provides both **built-in location-based proxies** and **custom proxy support**, giving you full flexibility depending on your scraping needs.

## How to Configure Proxy Settings

Follow these steps to set up a proxy for your scraper:

### Step 1: Open Your Scraper Settings

1. Go to your **Scrapers** page by clicking the **triangle icon** in the left sidebar.  
2. Select the scraper you want to configure.  
3. Click **Settings → Proxy Settings**.


### Step 2: Enable and Configure Proxy

In the **Proxy Settings** panel, you can select one of the following options:

#### Option 1: Use MrScraper’s Built-in Proxy

1. Toggle **Enable Proxy**.  
2. Under **Proxy Type**, choose **MrScraper Proxy**.  
3. Select your desired **location/region** from the dropdown list.  
   - Each region provides a local IP address, ideal for geo-targeted scraping.  
4. Click **Save Settings**.

<Callout title="Note">
The scraper will now route traffic through the selected country’s proxy network, allowing access to region-specific or restricted content.
</Callout>


#### Option 2: Use Custom Proxy
<Callout title="Warning" type="warning">
Only use proxies from trusted providers to ensure your data remains secure.
</Callout>
1. Toggle **Enable Proxy**.  
2. Under **Proxy Type**, choose **Custom Proxy**.  
3. Enter your proxy URL in one of the following formats: `http://username:password@host:port` or `https://host:port`.

4. Click **Save Settings** to apply your configuration.

<Callout title="Tip" type="success">
Use your own proxy if you have an existing provider, private IP pool, or dedicated proxy infrastructure.
</Callout>
